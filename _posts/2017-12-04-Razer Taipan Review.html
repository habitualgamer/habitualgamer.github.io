As a lefty, I’ve always struggled with finding the right mouse/keyboard setup for PC Gaming. It was even harder when certain games don’t support custom keybinds. Now times have changed, and gaming as a lefty has been much easier. In the past, the biggest hurdle was finding a gaming mouse that’s compatible with the left hand. Most mice created were specifically for a right-hander. Fortunately, there are mice available that cater to both sides. For me, the best of the bunch is the Razer Taipan.

My goal for a gaming mouse was simple: An ambidextrous form factor, and an adequate amount of buttons. Not as crazy as the Razer Naga, but something better than a standard three-button mouse. The Taipan comes with 9 programmable buttons, which is more than enough for what I need to do. Many MMORPGs like World of Warcraft have 20+ keys you need to press at any given time. This mouse helps free some of those keybinds, making abilities more accessible. Clicking feels responsive and there’s never a sense of delay from click to action. Even playing games like StarCraft and Cities: Skylines, where your main control is clicking, feels greats. The mouse movement is seamless, boasting an 8500 dpi 4g laser sensor for that insane precision. I never really needed to go that high, but it’s there for those that may find it useful.

The overall look is what sold me. I’m not a fan of those transformer, robotic-looking mice. I’m a simple guy, so a mouse with a simple, yet sleek design was something I was seeking. It has a sexy ambidextrous look with the flashing green Razer logo glowing on the front of the mouse. The scroll button also glows a vibrant green color, which makes the whole presentation subtle, yet appealing. The Taipan also comes with a tough beaded cord for that long-lasting durability. The rubber contoured grip on either side of the mouse helps with maintaining a comfortable grip for those long gaming sessions.

The only gripe I have with the mouse is its size. It’s a standard-sized mouse, which is fine but may be considered small for hardcore gamers. Because of how the buttons are placed, reaching the side buttons felt awkward at times. I believe this is one of the flaws of having a symmetrical design. A mouse specifically for the right or left hand will have a much better button placement, but the Taipan does what it can to accommodate both lefties and righties.

I’ve had this mouse for years, had the cords twisted out and everything, and it’s still going strong. For those lefty gamers looking for a gaming mouse that suits their needs, I highly recommend this one. It’s simple, yet sexy design, a multitude of buttons, tight precision, and great support via Razor Synapse makes this a must buy for gamers of all levels, regardless of what hand you use.
